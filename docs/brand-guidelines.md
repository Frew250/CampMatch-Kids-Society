# Brand Guidelines — Camp Match Kids Society

The single source of truth for **who we are and how we sound**. Read this before
writing copy or designing anything. If a choice isn't covered here, it should be
*added* here once decided — never re-guessed.

> Companion docs: [design-system.md](./design-system.md) (how it looks),
> [content-guide.md](./content-guide.md) (approved words & messaging).

---

## What we are

Camp Match Kids Society helps kids whose families can't afford it get to summer
camp — we cover fees and work with camps to open up free and discounted spots.

**Barbecue version (use this, not grant-speak):**
> "We help kids whose families can't swing the cost get to summer camp — we cover
> fees, and we work with camps to open up free or discounted spots."

## The two entities (keep distinct)

- **CampMatch.ca** — the for-profit marketplace ("find the right camp").
- **Camp Match Kids Society** — a separate **BC nonprofit**, the charitable sibling
  ("make sure every kid gets one"). Same founders, same brand family, **two legally
  distinct organisations.**
- Visually a clear **sibling**; in **copy**, always distinct — never let anyone think
  a donation goes to the for-profit.

## Where we are (stage)

Pre-launch, **incorporating** (BC name reservation active; not yet incorporated; not
yet a registered charity). Early traction is **real and present-tense**: a board in
place, camps already offering spots, supporters already offering money. Frame as
*"this is already happening,"* not a someday plan.

## Audience (who we're convincing, in order)

1. **Partner camps & supporters** — already warm; the proof the model works.
2. **Grant-makers** — later (month 4+).
3. **Families** — dignity-first, modest "tell us about your kid." Don't over-collect
   demand we can't yet serve.
- The board is **complete and private** — not a site audience, not named publicly.

## Voice & register

- First person **"we."** Plain, confident, **present tense.** Not grant-speak.
- **Warm but grown-up & credible** — "a serious organisation run by people who
  genuinely like kids," NOT a playful kids' brand.
- **Guardrail:** *warm and serious, not serious-instead-of-warm.* Warmth lives in the
  photography and the words; restraint lives in the layout and type. Don't let
  "credible" tip into cold/corporate.
- First-three-seconds feeling (priority order): **Trustworthy. Warm. Real.**

## Dignity-first (non-negotiable)

Centre the **kid and the summer**, never the family's finances.
- **Avoid:** "low-income," "qualify," "eligible," "underprivileged," means-test language.
- First contact is **"tell us about your kid,"** not "prove you need help." An open
  door, not an application.

## Legal guardrails (these bind the copy)

- Say **"donations"** — but NOT "tax-deductible" / receipts until CRA charitable
  registration is in hand. Don't promise receipts we can't issue.
- Say **"incorporating"** (in progress), not "incorporated," until filed.
- No invented stats / no claimed placement track record (no kids placed yet).

## Credibility — ours, not borrowed

Our credibility comes from the **Society's own traction**. Do **not** lean on the
CampMatch business's assets on this site: the Pedalheads partnership, the registration
platform, provider data integrations, or the company's advisors. What's ours to state
(present tense): a board in place, camps already offering spots, supporters stepping up.
